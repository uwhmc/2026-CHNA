What is the Community Health Needs Assessment?

Health is shaped by far more than medical care. The Community Health Needs Assessment identifies the most pressing health needs of Harvey County residents through a systematic, evidence-based process — and looks closely at how social drivers of health shape outcomes.

Built on collaboration, transparency, and evidence-based practice, the 2026 assessment examines both the current and future health needs of the community. It highlights Harvey County’s assets alongside its priority health issues, giving residents, providers, and partners a common picture to work from.

The findings don’t sit on a shelf. They drove the creation of a Community Health Improvement Plan covering July 2026 through June 2029 — a shared roadmap with specific goals, action steps, timeframes, and lead agencies for each priority. Stakeholders used the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services’ Healthy People 2030 framework to set measurable targets.

How the needs were determined

A diverse core team of lead agencies guided the assessment, combining first-hand community voices with trusted regional and national data.

Primary data

Community survey and focus groups

A countywide health survey in English and Spanish, plus 15 focus groups intentionally designed to reach voices often left out — including residents in recovery, Spanish-speaking residents, and those with limited formal education.

Secondary data

Trusted regional and national sources

Statistics from the U.S. Census Bureau, the CDC, the Kansas Department of Health and Environment, and dashboards such as Kansas Health Matters and County Health Rankings.

Community feedback

A “data walk”

This year the team used a data walk to share early results with the core team and the community and gather feedback across age, ethnicity, race, and income.

A diverse team then weighed the evidence to select the county’s health priorities, which became the foundation for the three-year improvement plan.

Four health priorities for 2026–2029

These four priorities focus Harvey County’s health initiatives over the next three years and drove the goals in the improvement plan.

1

Mental and Behavioral Health

Increase treatment for adults with mental illness by connecting residents to resources through employers, chambers of commerce, and a countywide resource directory.

2

Chronic Disease Prevention

Build a collaborative communication plan to raise awareness of services for diabetes, heart disease, and cancer.

3

Prescription Access

Reduce the number of people who cannot afford or access needed medications by understanding barriers and training navigators to connect residents to assistance.

4

Social and Economic Factors

Improve transportation, expand childcare availability and affordability, and increase access to affordable housing.
